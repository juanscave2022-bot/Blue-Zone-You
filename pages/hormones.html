Our Methodology

Five Pillars of Hormone Health

We evaluate and optimize your hormonal system across five interconnected pillars. Imbalance in one affects all the others, which is why our approach is always comprehensive.

Sex Hormones

Testosterone, estrogen, and progesterone form the foundation of vitality. We measure free and total levels, SHBG, and DHT to understand not just how much hormone your body produces, but how effectively it uses what's available. Genetic variants in aromatase and 5-alpha reductase pathways determine your ideal protocol.

Thyroid Axis

Your thyroid sets the metabolic pace for every cell in your body. We go far beyond standard TSH testing to measure Free T3, Free T4, Reverse T3, and thyroid antibodies. Genetic variants in deiodinase enzymes and thyroid receptor genes tell us exactly how to optimize your thyroid function at the cellular level.

5 markers analyzed

Adrenal & Cortisol

Chronic stress dysregulates your entire hormonal cascade. We measure cortisol rhythm, DHEA-S, and pregnenolone to assess adrenal output. When the stress axis is overactive, it steals precursors from sex hormone production, creating a downstream deficit that no amount of testosterone or estrogen can fix without addressing the root.

4 markers analyzed

Insulin & Metabolic Hormones

Insulin resistance is one of the most common hidden drivers of hormone imbalance. Elevated insulin suppresses SHBG, raises free estrogen in men, and disrupts ovulation in women. We monitor fasting insulin, HbA1c, and leptin to ensure your metabolic hormones are supporting rather than sabotaging your protocol.

3 markers analyzed

Growth Hormone & IGF-1

Growth hormone declines roughly 14% per decade after age 30. We measure IGF-1 and assess pituitary function to determine whether peptide therapy, lifestyle interventions, or targeted supplementation can restore youthful GH pulsatility. Optimizing this axis accelerates recovery, fat loss, and tissue repair.

Thyroid Support

Your thyroid controls the metabolic rate of every cell in your body. When it underperforms, even mildly, you experience fatigue, weight gain, hair loss, cold intolerance, and mental sluggishness. Standard medicine often misses subclinical thyroid dysfunction because they only test TSH. We run the full panel: TSH, Free T3, Free T4, Reverse T3, TPO antibodies, and thyroglobulin antibodies.

Genetic analysis of deiodinase enzymes (DIO1, DIO2) reveals whether you're a poor converter of T4 to the active T3 form. For these patients, combination therapy with both T4 and T3 produces dramatically better outcomes than levothyroxine alone. We also address nutritional cofactors like selenium, zinc, and iodine based on your nutrigenomic profile.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if my hormones are low?

Common signs include persistent fatigue, unexplained weight gain, brain fog, low libido, poor sleep, muscle loss, irritability, and slow recovery from exercise. If you're experiencing several of these symptoms, a comprehensive hormone panel can confirm whether your levels are suboptimal. Many people have "normal" levels that are far from optimal.

Are bioidentical hormones safe?

Bioidentical hormones are molecularly identical to what your body produces naturally, meaning they're metabolized through the same enzymatic pathways. When properly dosed and monitored with regular bloodwork, they have an excellent safety profile. Our genetic testing adds an extra layer of safety by identifying how you metabolize hormones and where risks might lie.

How soon will I feel results?

Most patients notice initial improvements in energy, sleep, and mood within 2-3 weeks. The full effects of hormone optimization, including body composition changes, cognitive clarity, and sexual function, typically manifest within 4-6 weeks. We recheck labs at 6 weeks to confirm levels are in the optimal range and adjust if needed.

Do I need to be on hormones forever?

It depends on your situation. Some patients use hormone therapy as a long-term optimization strategy, while others use it as a bridge while we address root causes like stress, sleep, nutrition, and metabolic dysfunction. Your clinician will discuss the long-term plan during your consultation and adjust your approach as your health evolves.

What's the difference between your approach and a standard TRT clinic?

Most TRT clinics test total testosterone, write a prescription, and send you on your way. We test 15+ biomarkers, analyze your hormone metabolism genes, consider the full hormonal cascade (thyroid, adrenals, insulin, growth hormone), and build a protocol that accounts for your entire system. Our protocols are genetically guided and continuously optimized with regular monitoring.

Can women benefit from testosterone therapy?

Absolutely. Women produce testosterone naturally, and optimal levels are essential for energy, libido, muscle tone, bone density, and cognitive function. When levels drop during perimenopause and menopause, low-dose bioidentical testosterone can be transformative. We use precise, genetically-guided dosing to restore levels without masculinizing side effects.
